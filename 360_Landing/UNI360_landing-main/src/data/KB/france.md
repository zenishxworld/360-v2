## 1. Country Overview

- **Official name:** French Republic (République française)
- **Capital:** Paris
- **Major cities:** Lyon, Marseille, Toulouse, Lille, Bordeaux, Strasbourg
- **Population:** ~68.4 million
- **Currency:** Euro (€)
- **Official language:** French
- **Time zone:** Central European Time (CET, UTC+1) / CEST (UTC+2)
- **GDP:** ~$3.1 trillion USD (Nominal)
- **Cost of Living Index:** 74.2 (Moderate-High)
- **HDI:** 0.910 (Very High)
- **Safety:** 67th globally (Global Peace Index)
- **Quality of Life:** Top 15 globally
- **Global Innovation Ranking:** 11th (WIPO)
- **Education Ranking:** 6th globally for higher education systems
- **International students:** ~410,000 (targeting 500,000 by 2027)
- **Climate:** Temperate — 4 distinct seasons. Mediterranean in the south, oceanic in the west.
- **Culture:** Deeply rooted in arts, philosophy, gastronomy, literature. Public life follows *laïcité* (secularism).
- **Healthcare:** PUMA (Protection Universelle Maladie) covers all residents including international students.
- **Infrastructure:** World-class. TGV high-speed rail, dense metro/bus networks in cities.

## 2. Why Study in France?

### Pros

- Very low tuition fees at public universities compared to the US, UK, Australia
- Up to 24 months post-study work visa for Indian Master's graduates
- World-leading sectors: AI, aerospace, luxury, mathematics
- Subsidized healthcare, transport, and meals
- High return on investment — quality education at a fraction of Anglophone costs
- €54 billion "France 2030" investment plan creates research and job opportunities

### Cons

- Complex and slow administrative bureaucracy
- CAF housing aid (APL) eliminated for non-EU students from July 2026
- B1/B2 French is strongly needed for job market entry
- Highly competitive and expensive housing in major cities

### Best for students who want:

- STEM (especially mathematics and AI)
- Luxury brand management
- Aerospace engineering
- Culinary arts
- A path to European work and residency

## 3. Education System

### LMD Framework (Licence, Master, Doctorat)

Fits the European standard. Your credits transfer across Europe.

### Credit System (ECTS)

- 1 full academic year = 60 ECTS credits
- 1 semester = 30 ECTS credits

### Degree Structure

| Degree | Duration | ECTS | Notes |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Licence (Bachelor's) | 3 years | 180 | General/theoretical focus |
| Master's | 2 years | 120 | Split into M1 (year 1) and M2 (year 2). M2 requires a mandatory internship |
| Doctorat (PhD) | 3–4 years | — | Operates as a salaried employment contract, not just study |

### Academic Calendar

- **Start:** September
- **End:** June
- Two semesters per year

### Grading System (20-point scale)

Grade inflation is essentially nonexistent. A perfect 20 is extraordinarily rare.

| French Grade (/20) | ECTS Grade | US GPA Equivalent | Meaning |
|--------------------|------------|-------------------|---------|
| 16.0 – 20.0 | A | A+ / 4.0 | Excellent / Perfection |
| 14.0 – 15.9 | B | A / 4.0 | Very Good |
| 12.0 – 13.9 | C | B+ / 3.3 | Good |
| 11.0 – 11.9 | D | B / 3.0 | Average / Passable |
| 10.0 – 10.9 | E | C / 2.0 | Sufficient / Passing |
| 0.0 – 9.9 | F | F / 0.0 | Fail |

### Type of Institutions

- **Public universities:** Unselective entry (but demanding once enrolled). Focus on theory and research. Low tuition.
- **Grandes Écoles:** Highly selective, elite. Deep corporate ties. Higher tuition. Engineering, business, public administration.
- **Private schools:** Set their own fees. Common for business, engineering, and specialized fields.

## 5. Bachelor's Degree (Licence)

### Admission Requirements

- Completed secondary education equivalent to French Baccalauréat (e.g., 12th Grade CBSE/ICSE)
- Apply through **Parcoursup** (domestic portal) or **Études en France (EEF)** (for students from 60+ countries including India)

### Academic Requirements

- Consistent secondary school transcripts
- No strict minimum percentage, but competitive programs look for strong grades

### Language Requirements

| Program Language | Minimum Score |
|-----------------|---------------|
| French-taught | DELF B2 (mandatory) |
| English-taught | IELTS 6.0+ |

### Application Timeline

- **October:** Applications open
- **Mid-December:** EEF application deadline (strict)
- **Visa process:** Follows after admission

### Required Documents

- Academic transcripts
- Language test scores
- Motivation letter (Statement of Purpose)
- Letters of Recommendation

### Classes Internationales (for Indian students)

- Designed for top high school graduates with **zero French (A0)**
- One fully immersive preparatory year in France to learn French
- After reaching B2, you transition into standard undergraduate programs
- Major pathway for Indian students wanting to study in French without prior language training

### Tuition

**€2,895/year** for non-EU students (2026)

## 6. Master's Degree

### Admission Requirements

- Recognized Bachelor's degree (180 ECTS minimum)
- Strong alignment between your Bachelor's field and intended Master's

### Academic Expectations

- Minimum GPA equivalent to 12/20 (~70%, 3.0/4.0 GPA, or solid Second Division/B average)
- Statement of Purpose (SOP)
- Letters of Recommendation (LOR)
- Resume (CV)
- Work experience is NOT mandatory (except for executive MBAs)

### Language Requirements

| Program Language | Minimum Score |
|-----------------|---------------|
| English-taught | IELTS 6.5–7.0 |
| French-taught | DELF B2 (minimum), DALF C1 (preferred) |

### Application Timeline

- Via EEF platform
- Deadlines: January to March (vary by program)

### Curriculum

- **M1 (Year 1):** Coursework-focused
- **M2 (Year 2):** Specialized coursework + **mandatory 4–6 month internship** (research or industry)
- The internship serves as a direct pipeline into the job market

### Tuition

**€3,941/year** for non-EU students (2026)

## 7. PhD (Doctorat)

### Key Facts

- Administered by specialized Doctoral Schools (*Écoles Doctorales*)
- Eligibility: Completed Master's degree (120 ECTS)
- Duration: 3–4 years
- The PhD operates as a salaried employment contract, not pure study

### Admission Process

1. You do NOT just apply to a program
2. You must **find a thesis supervisor** (*Directeur de thèse*) first
3. You must **secure funding** (institutional or external) before enrollment

### Funding

- **Contrat Doctoral:** In STEM fields, you are legally required to hold this contract. It provides a monthly salary as an employee.
- **France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship:** €2,100/month (from January 2026)

### Tuition

**€380/year** for ALL nationalities — PhD students are exempt from fee hikes.

## 8. Admission Process (Step by Step)

This is the EEF (*Études en France*) procedure — mandatory for students from 60+ countries including India.

| Step | Timeline | What to Do |
|------|----------|------------|
| 1. Course Selection | Aug–Oct | Search the Campus France catalog. Find programs matching your profile. |
| 2. Language Tests | Sept–Nov | Take IELTS, TOEFL, or DELF/DALF. |
| 3. Document Prep | Oct–Nov | Gather transcripts, CV, SOP, LORs. |
| 4. EEF Profile | Nov–Dec | Register on the *Études en France* portal. Enter personal and academic data. |
| 5. Submit Applications | Dec–Feb | Choose up to 7 programs. Submit through the portal. |
| 6. Campus France Interview | Feb–Mar | Mandatory academic interview. Evaluates your motivation and preparedness. |
| 7. Accept Offer & Pay | Mar–May | Review offers. Accept your preferred university on EEF. Pay tuition deposits. |
| 8. Find Housing | May–Jun | Secure verified accommodation. This is required before you can apply for the visa. |
| 9. Visa Application | Jun–Jul | Apply for VLS-TS via France-Visas and TLS/VFS Global. |
| 10. Travel & Validate | Aug–Sept | Arrive in France. Validate your VLS-TS on the ANEF portal within 3 months. |

> **Important:** Campus France processes your file, but the university makes the final admission decision.

- Previously, universities could waive the higher non-EU fees and charge domestic rates

- **CROUS need-based grants:** Mostly for French and EU citizens only.
- **Eiffel scholarship holders are exempt from the CVEC fee.**

## 13. Student Visa (VLS-TS)

### What is the VLS-TS?

A **Long-Stay Visa serving as a Residence Permit**. Non-EU nationals need this to study in France.

### Financial Requirements

| Requirement | Amount | Details |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Legal minimum | **€615/month (€7,380/year)** | Statutory floor |
| Paris (recommended) | **€1,000–€1,200/month** | Consulates expect higher for Paris |

### Accepted Proof of Funds

- **3–6 months of personal bank statements**
- Official scholarship award letter
- Notarized financial guarantee from a sponsor (parent)
- Approved education loan disbursement letter

> **No blocked account needed.** France does not require a blocked account like Germany.

### Required Documents

- Valid passport
- Unconditional acceptance letter from a French institution
- Flight itinerary or reservation (does NOT need to be fully paid)
- 2 biometric photos (EU format: 35x45mm, white background)
- **Proof of accommodation in France for at least the first 90 days** (lease, hotel booking, or guarantor letter)
- Medical insurance covering health/hospitalization until you register for French Social Security
- Campus France interview certificate

### Visa Application Fee

**€99**

### Processing Time

Typically **15–30 days** during peak summer (July–August).

### VLS-TS Validation (After Arrival)

1. You must validate your visa within **3 months of arrival**
2. Go to the **ANEF portal** (administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr)
3. Enter your visa details
4. Pay the **€75 tax stamp** (*timbre fiscal*) online

> **Failure to validate = you lose legal residency.** You cannot re-enter the Schengen Area if you leave.

### Travel Rights

Your VLS-TS allows **unrestricted travel across all 27 Schengen countries**.

### What if your visa is rejected?

- You receive a refusal letter
- You can appeal to the commission in Nantes (takes months)
- Rejection is usually due to: insufficient funds, weak academic motivation, or fraudulent documents

### Key Rules

- You cannot enter France **before the start date** on your visa sticker
- You must apply for the visa **from your country of legal residence**
- You **cannot convert a tourist visa** to a student visa inside France
- If you drop out, the university reports you to the prefecture, and your VLS-TS is cancelled

## 14. Work While Studying

### How Many Hours Can You Work?

- **964 hours per year**
- That's about **20 hours per week** (60% of full-time)
- Your VLS-TS student visa **automatically serves as your work permit** — no separate permit needed

### Full-Time During Holidays

Yes, you can work full-time during holidays as long as you don't exceed the 964-hour annual limit.

### Minimum Wage (SMIC)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Gross hourly wage (as of June 1, 2026) | **€12.31/hour** |
| Monthly baseline (full-time) | €1,867.02 |
| Max annual earnings (964 hours) | ~€11,866 |

### Taxes on Student Wages

- Student part-time wages at SMIC level fall **below the income tax threshold**
- Generally **tax-exempt**, though social security contributions (CSG/CRDS ~22–25%) are deducted from gross pay
- You still must file an annual tax return each May

### Best Part-Time Jobs

- English tutoring
- Retail
- Hospitality (cafes, restaurants)
- Campus assistant roles

> **Language note:** Customer-facing jobs require French. English-only jobs are mostly tutoring or tech startups.

### Important Rules

- You can hold **multiple part-time jobs** as long as the combined total ≤ 964 hours/year
- **Freelancing is NOT allowed** on a student visa — only salaried work
- **Alternance** (work-study program where a company pays tuition + salary) is very hard for non-EU first-year students due to visa rules

## 15. Internships (Stages)

### Why Internships Matter

Internships are the **pedagogical core of the French Master's degree**. They bridge academic theory with the job market. The M2 year almost always includes a mandatory 4–6 month internship.

### Payment Rules

| Duration | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| ≤ 2 months (or ≤ 309 hours) | Can be unpaid |
| **> 2 months (or > 309 hours)** | **Legally must pay minimum gratification** |

### Minimum Internship Pay (2026)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Hourly minimum | **€4.50/hour** |
| Full-time month (154 hours) | **~€693/month** |
| Tax status | **100% exempt** from income tax AND social charges |

### Legal Framework

Internships require a **convention de stage** — a mandatory legal agreement signed by:
1. You (the student)
2. Your university
3. The employer

### How to Find Internships

- University portals
- LinkedIn
- Welcome to the Jungle
- Networking events

### Do internship hours count toward your 964-hour work limit?

**No.** Academic internships are integrated into your curriculum and are separate from your legal work hours.

## 16. Graduate Jobs & Post-Study Work

### The Graduate Job Market

- **Strong demand in:** AI, data architecture, sustainable engineering, supply chain, luxury brand management
- **Standard contract:** CDI (*Contrat à Durée Indéterminée*) — a permanent contract with exceptional job security
- **Entry-level salaries for Master's graduates:** €35,000–€45,000 gross/year (higher in Paris consulting, banking, deep tech)

### Post-Study Work Permit (RECE)

The **Carte de séjour "recherche d'emploi / création d'entreprise" (RECE)** — formerly called APS.

| Feature | Standard Non-EU | Indian Students |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Duration | **12 months** (non-renewable) | **24 months** (12+12, renewable once) |
| Work rights | Full-time, no hourly restrictions | Full-time, no hourly restrictions |
| Can start a business? | Yes (explicitly allowed) | Yes |
| Can Bachelor's grads get it? | Only Professional Bachelor's (Licence Pro) | Only Professional Bachelor's (Licence Pro) |
| Application fee | €150 | €150 |

### Converting RECE to a Work Permit (Salarié)

To switch from RECE to a standard work permit:

1. Your job must be **related to your degree**
2. Your salary must meet the **1.5x SMIC rule**

### 1.5x SMIC Threshold (2026)

| Calculation | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| SMIC monthly (June 2026) | €1,867.02 |
| 1.5x SMIC = minimum gross monthly salary needed | **€2,734.55** |
| Annual equivalent | **~€32,814** |

> If your job offer pays below this threshold, your work permit application will be rejected.

### Talent Passport

- A **4-year premium work permit** for highly skilled workers
- Requires salary above **€39,582/year**
- Bypasses standard labor market tests
- Allows immediate family reunification
- Speeds up PR pathway

### The 5-Year Indian Alumni Visa

> **Important clarification:** This is a **short-stay Schengen circulation visa** allowing multiple 90-day tourist visits over 5 years. It is **NOT a work permit** and does **NOT** confer long-term residency or employment rights.

### What if you don't find a job in 12 months?

You must leave France unless you:
- Enroll in another degree program, OR
- Switch to another visa category

## 17. Permanent Residency (PR) & Citizenship

### Permanent Residency (Carte de Résident)

- **10-year renewable card**
- Eligibility requires **5 consecutive years of uninterrupted legal residency**
- Must demonstrate:
  - Stable, sufficient income (minimum SMIC)
  - A2/B1 French proficiency
  - Integration into French society
- **Stable CDI employment is a core requirement** — you cannot get PR without a job

### Citizenship (Naturalization)

| Pathway | Residency Requirement |
|---------|----------------------|
| Standard | 5 years continuous legal residency |
| **Reduced (with French degree)** | **2 years** — if you completed 2 years of higher education leading to a French degree |

### Citizenship Requirements

- **B1 French level** (certified, tested via integration interview)
- Pass an interview on French history, culture, and *laïcité* (secularism)
- Demonstrate professional integration (stable CDI employment)
- **Dual citizenship is fully permitted** — France has no restrictions

## 20. Healthcare for Students

### PUMA (Protection Universelle Maladie)

- Universal healthcare for all residents including international students
- Covers **~70% of standard medical costs**
- Must register via the **Ameli portal** (etudiant-etranger.ameli.fr) upon arrival
- Funded indirectly through the **€105 CVEC fee**

### Mutuelle (Top-Up Insurance)

- Covers the remaining **~30%** not covered by PUMA
- Cost: **€200–€600/year** depending on coverage level
- Strongly recommended — especially for dental care, which PUMA covers only partially

### Emergency Services

- **Dial 15** for SAMU (emergency medical service)
- Emergency care is fully covered by the public system
- **Dial 112** for general European emergency
- **Dial 17** for police
- **Dial 18** for fire/rescue

### Carte Vitale

The physical green card holding your French Social Security info for medical billing. You receive it after registering with Ameli.

### Vaccinations

Standard childhood vaccines are expected. Check specific requirements before travel.

### English-Speaking Doctors

Platforms like **Doctolib** let you filter doctors by languages they speak.

## 21. Accommodation

### Options

| Type | Monthly Cost | Details |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **CROUS residence** (state-subsidized) | €200–€400 | Very cheap, but very hard to get. Priority for scholarship holders. |
| **Private studio** | €550–€1,200 | Most common. Price varies hugely by city. |
| **Colocation** (shared apartment) | €350–€800 | Most affordable option in big cities. |
| **Homestay** | Varies | Living with a French family. Good for language immersion. |

### CAF Housing Aid (APL) — CRITICAL 2026 UPDATE

| Before July 2026 | From July 2026 |
|------------------|----------------|
| All students could get €100–€280/month | **Non-EU students without a French state scholarship are excluded** |
| Significant help with rent | You must cover full rent yourself |

### How to Find Housing

- **Studapart** (popular for international students)
- **Leboncoin** (French Craigslist)
- **Immojeune** (student-focused)
- **Lokaviz** (CROUS listings)
- Facebook groups (but beware of scams)

### Guarantor (Garant)

Landlords demand someone who will pay rent if you default. International students use:

- **Visale:** Free state-backed guarantee service. Acts as your guarantor. Highly recommended.
- Visale works for **colocations** too, as long as your name is on the lease.

### Security Deposit (Dépôt de Garantie)

Usually **1 or 2 months of rent**, paid upfront. Returned at the end of the lease (minues documented damages on the *état des lieux*).

### Home Insurance (Assurance Habitation)

**Mandatory.** You cannot sign a lease or get keys without it.

### Lease Termination (Préavis)

- For furnished student apartments: **1 month** notice period by law
- Unfurnished: usually 3 months

### Scam Warning

Never wire money via Western Union/MoneyGram before seeing the apartment and signing a lease.

### What is a Studio?

A small apartment where the bedroom, living room, and kitchen are in a single room.

### Utilities

- *"Charges comprises"* means water/building fees are included in rent
- Electricity and internet are usually **extra**
- **Council tax** (*taxe d'habitation*) has been abolished for primary residences — students don't pay it

## 23. Banking & Finance

### Do you need a French bank account?

**Yes.** You need a local IBAN (called a **RIB** — *Relevé d'Identité Bancaire*) to:
- Pay rent
- Receive salary
- Get healthcare reimbursements
- Handle CAF payments (if eligible)

### How to Open an Account

Documents required:
1. Passport
2. Valid VLS-TS visa
3. Proof of French address

### Bank Options

| Type | Examples | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Traditional banks | BNP Paribas, Société Générale, Crédit Agricole | Offer student packages |
| Digital challengers | Revolut, N26 | Fast onboarding, but French institutions sometimes prefer domestic "FR" IBANs |

### Money Transfers from Home

Use **Wise** or **Remitly** for better exchange rates than traditional bank SWIFT transfers.

## 24. Taxes

### Do students pay taxes?

- You must **file a tax return every year in May** (even with zero income)
- **Request a Tax Number** (*Numéro Fiscal*) from your local *Centre des Finances Publiques*
- Part-time student wages at SMIC level are generally **too low to trigger income tax**
- However, social contributions (**CSG/CRDS ~22–25%**) are deducted directly from gross pay

### Funds from Home

Money transferred from parents for living expenses is **NOT subject to French income tax.**

## 25. Safety

- **General safety:** High. Violent crime is statistically rare.
- **Primary risk:** Pickpocketing in the Paris metro and major tourist areas. Organized syndicates operate here.
- **Vigipirate:** National security alert system. You will see armed military patrols at train stations and airports.
- **Emergency numbers:**
  - 112 — General European Emergency
  - 17 — Police
  - 15 — SAMU (Medical)
  - 18 — Fire/Rescue

### Discrimination

France is highly diverse. While discrimination exists, the legal framework against it is robust.

## 29. Latest Updates & Policy Shifts (2025–2026)

### 1. SMIC Increase (June 1, 2026)

- Gross hourly SMIC: **€12.31/hour**
- This raised the **1.5x SMIC threshold** for work permit conversion to **€2,734.55/month**
- Student part-time earnings remain below the tax threshold

### 2. Internship Pay Increase (January 1, 2026)

- Minimum hourly internship gratification: **€4.50/hour**
- Full-time intern earns ~**€693/month tax-free**

### 3. Tuition Fee Exemptions Ended

- *Bienvenue en France* is now strictly enforced
- Non-EU students pay **€2,895 (Bachelor's)** and **€3,941 (Master's)**
- Very few waivers remain (only refugees, doctoral students, specific bilateral partners)

### 4. CAF Housing Aid Eliminated (July 2026)

- **Non-EU students without a French state scholarship lose APL/ALS eligibility**
- €100–€280/month removed from student budgets
- This is the most significant budget impact for international students in 2026

### 5. Eiffel Scholarship Stipend Increase (January 2026)

- Master's: **€1,200/month** (up from previous rate)
- PhD: **€2,100/month** (up from previous rate)

### 6. Classes Internationales Expansion (2025–2026)

- The India-France bilateral program is scaling up
- Premier pathway for Indian students with **zero French (A0)** to enter Francophone higher education
- Fully immersive preparatory year in France
