## 📍 Why Study in Italy?

### ✅ Pros
- **Low tuition fees** at public universities — international students pay the same as locals
- **English-taught programs** are growing fast in STEM, business, and design
- **DSU scholarships** — need-based grants covering tuition, housing, meals, and cash stipends
- **Post-study work** — student visas can convert to work permits without quota restrictions (Law 187/2024)
- **High-quality research** — Italian universities lead in European Horizon funding (physics, AI, engineering)
- **Safe environment** — ranked high on Global Peace Index
- **Rich culture & lifestyle** — food, art, history, and Mediterranean climate

### ❌ Cons
- **Housing shortage** in Milan, Rome, and other big cities — rents are high and dorms are limited
- **Bureaucracy** — processes like getting a residence permit (Permesso di Soggiorno) and tax code (Codice Fiscale) can be slow
- **Healthcare cost increase** — SSN registration for non-scholarship students is now €700/year (was €149.77)

### 🧑‍🎓 Best Student Profile
Independent, adaptable, academically strong students focused on STEM, design, or business who can navigate administrative processes.

## 🎓 Education System

Italy follows the **Bologna Process** (EU-wide system), so your degree is recognized across Europe.

### Degree Structure

| Degree | Italian Name | Duration | Credits (ECTS/CFU) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|----------|-------------------|-------|
| **Bachelor's** | Laurea Triennale | 3 years | 180 ECTS | Requires 12 years of prior schooling |
| **Master's** | Laurea Magistrale | 2 years | 120 ECTS | Requires relevant Bachelor's (180 ECTS) |
| **PhD** | Dottorato di Ricerca | 3 years | — | Competitive entrance exam (concorso) |
| **Professional Master's** | Master Universitario (I/II livello) | 1 year | 60 ECTS | Career-focused, usually doesn't lead to PhD |

### Key Terms
- **CFU = ECTS** — They're the same thing. 1 CFU = 1 ECTS = 25 hours of student work.
- **Academic calendar** — Starts late September/October, ends June/July. Two semesters (Autumn & Spring).

### Grading System
- **Exam grades:** 0–30 scale. Minimum pass = **18/30**. Maximum = **30 e lode** (30 with honors).
- **Final degree grade:** 66–110 scale. Minimum pass = **66/110**. Maximum = **110 e lode**.
- **GPA conversion:** (Your GPA ÷ Max GPA) × 110.

## 📘 Bachelor's Degree (Laurea Triennale)

### Eligibility
- **12 years of schooling** is mandatory. If your country has only 11 years, you need a foundation year first.
- **Minimum 60%** in secondary school for most programs.
- Competitive programs (engineering, medicine) have **entrance exams** (e.g., IMAT for medicine).

### Language Requirements
- **English-taught programs:** IELTS 5.5–6.0 (or equivalent: TOEFL iBT 78–80, PTE 59, Duolingo 100–110)
- **Italian-taught programs:** B2 Italian certification (CILS, CELI)
- **MOI (Medium of Instruction) waiver** — universities may accept proof your previous degree was in English. But **consulates can still demand test scores or interview you** for the visa.

### Application Process (2 Steps)
1. **Apply to the university** via their internal portal (Jan–May deadlines)
2. **Pre-enroll on Universitaly portal** (mandatory) — this links your admission to the consulate for visa processing

### Required Documents
- Legalized & apostilled transcripts and diploma
- Passport copy
- Language certificate (or MOI letter)
- CV (Europass format recommended)
- Statement of Purpose (SOP)
- Letters of Recommendation (LORs) — for Master's/PhD

### Tuition
- Public universities: **€0 (minimum €156 regional tax) to €4,000/year**
- Cheapest options: Southern universities (Calabria, Messina)

### Scholarships
- Bachelor's students are fully eligible for **DSU regional scholarships** (need-based) — see Scholarships section.

## 📗 Master's Degree (Laurea Magistrale)

### Eligibility
- Relevant Bachelor's degree with **minimum 180 ECTS**
- Your prior coursework must match the program's required **SSD (Scientific Disciplinary Sectors)**
- Minimum GPA equivalent to **6.5/10** (competitive programs may require higher)

### Language Requirements
- **IELTS 6.0–6.5** for English-taught programs
- **MOI waiver** is possible at the university level, but **consulates may still require formal test scores or conduct video interviews**
- Safest route: Have a formal test score even if the university waives it

### Application Materials
- SOP (Statement of Purpose) — very important for both admission and visa
- 2–3 LORs (Letters of Recommendation)
- Europass-format CV
- Work experience is **rarely mandatory** (except for MBA/executive programs)

### Deadlines
- **Rolling** from November to early June. Check each university's portal.

### Tuition
- Public universities: **€0–€4,000/year** (same progressive ISEE-based system as Bachelor's)

### Scholarships Available
- DSU regional grants
- University merit grants (e.g., Polimi Platinum/Gold/Bronze, UniTo Talents 4 UniTo)
- MAECI government grants

## 🔬 PhD (Dottorato di Ricerca)

### Eligibility
- Master's degree totaling **300 ECTS** (Bachelor's + Master's combined)
- Must apply through a **public competition (concorso)** — even if you have a professor's support

### Admission Process
1. Find a published call (bando) from a university
2. Submit a research proposal, transcripts, and other documents
3. Undergo an oral defense/interview
4. Contacting a potential supervisor **before applying** is strongly recommended

### Funding
- **Standard stipend:** ~€1,200–€1,500 net/month (€15,343–€19,000 gross/year)
- Includes INPS social security contributions
- Extra research grants for periods abroad (up to 50% more)
- **Teaching allowed:** Up to 40 paid hours/year without affecting your stipend

### Tuition
- Minimal (~€156/year). Most PhDs are fully funded.

## 💰 Scholarships & Financial Aid

### 1️⃣ DSU Regional Scholarships (Need-Based)

This is the **main scholarship for international students** in Italy. It's based on **family income, not grades**.

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Who gives it?** | Regional bodies (EDISU in Piedmont, ERGO in Emilia-Romagna, LAzioDisco in Lazio, etc.) |
| **Eligibility** | Need-based. Your family's ISEE must be below **€25,000–€30,000** |
| **Full benefits** | ISEE below **€13,560–€14,000** |
| **What you get** | ✅ Full tuition waiver + ✅ Subsidized/free dorm + ✅ Meal card (€0–€3/meal) + ✅ Cash stipend |
| **Cash stipend** | **€2,500–€7,900/year** (highest for "fuori sede" — students living away from home) |
| **Payment schedule** | Two installments: December and May |
| **Deadline** | Usually **September 30**. Late applications get **€50–€327 penalties** and risk losing dorm allocation |
| **Financial year** | For 2025–2026, you provide documents from the **2023 tax year** |

#### DSU Conditions & Penalties
- You must earn a **minimum number of ECTS credits each year** to keep the scholarship
- If you fail: You **lose the grant next year** AND must **refund the cash stipend**
- Fuori sede = away-from-home student (highest stipend)
- Pendolare = commuting student
- In sede = local student who lives with family

### 2️⃣ University Merit Scholarships

| University | Program | What You Get |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| **Politecnico di Milano** | Platinum | **€10,000/year** + full tuition waiver |
| **Politecnico di Milano** | Gold | **€5,000–€8,000/year** |
| **Politecnico di Milano** | Bronze | **€2,500/year** |
| **University of Turin** | Talents 4 UniTo | **€20,000** for 2 years (38 positions) |

### 3️⃣ Government & Targeted Scholarships

| Scholarship | Amount | Notes |
|------------|:------:|-------|
| **MAECI (Ministry of Foreign Affairs)** | €9,000/year | ❌ Cannot be stacked with DSU |
| **UNICORE 8.0** | €7,000/year | For refugee students (India, Ethiopia, Nigeria, etc.) |

### ⚠️ Stacking Rules
- **DSU + MAECI = ❌** Not allowed. Choose one.
- **DSU + University merit grant = ❌** Generally not allowed.
- **MAECI + University merit grant = ❌** Generally not allowed.

## ✈️ Student Visa (Type D – Long Stay)

### Financial Requirements (2025–2026)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Minimum funds** | **€6,947.33/year** (€534.41/month × 12) |
| **Bank statements** | **6 months** of original, bank-stamped statements |
| **Warning** | Sudden, unaged deposits are **routinely flagged as fraudulent** |
| **Education loan** | Accepted — provide the formal sanction letter |
| **Fixed Deposit (FD)** | Sometimes accepted, but liquid savings are preferred |

### Sponsorship Rules
- **Only parents or legal guardians** can sponsor (no uncles, aunts, etc.)
- Sponsor must provide:
  - **3 years** of Income Tax Returns (ITR)
  - **6 months** bank statements
  - Proof of employment/business
  - Notarized sponsorship letter (in Italian or English)

### Visa Application Process
1. Complete **Universitaly pre-enrollment** first
2. Submit Type D visa application via **VFS Global** or consulate
3. **Biometrics** collected at application center
4. **Processing time:** 30–90 days
5. **Visa fee:** ~€116

### Visa Interview (Mandatory in Many Countries)
- Countries like **India (Mumbai consulate)** require a **video interview**
- Officers assess: academic rationale, financial understanding, English skills, intent to return home
- **Poor interview performance = common rejection reason**

### ✅ Visa Success Factors
- Logical academic progression (no unexplained gaps)
- Strong Statement of Purpose
- Apostilled/legalized documents
- Clean, aged bank funds
- Prove ties to your home country (family, property, job prospects)

### ❌ Top Rejection Reasons
| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Expired passport** | Passport must be valid |
| **Fake/unaged funds** | Sudden deposits flagged as fraudulent |
| **Poor interview** | Can't explain your study plan or finances |
| **Weak home ties** | Officer believes you'll stay illegally |
| **Wrong visa type** | Applied for wrong category |
| **Incomplete documents** | Missing translations, apostilles, etc. |

### If Your Visa is Rejected
- You can **appeal within 60 days** to the Regional Administrative Court (TAR) in Lazio
- The process is **legally complex and expensive**
- Consider re-applying with stronger documentation instead

## 💼 Work While Studying

### Rules
| Rule | Limit |
|------|:-----:|
| **Weekly maximum** | 20 hours/week |
| **Annual maximum** | 1,040 hours/year |
| **During holidays** | Hours can be consolidated, but **annual cap still applies** |

### What You Need
- **Codice Fiscale (Tax Code)** — mandatory for any employment, bank account, or rental contract
- Employers must register you with **INPS** (National Social Security)

### Pay
- Italy has **no national minimum wage**
- Wages are set by **Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs)** — typically **€7–€9/hour gross**
- Law 144/2025 reinforces CBA minimums

### Popular Student Jobs
- Digital marketing
- Hospitality (restaurants, bars, hotels)
- English tutoring
- University collaborations ("150-hour program" — paid work within the university)

### Tax on Student Income
- Income up to **~€8,500/year** falls in the "no-tax area"
- You can file a tax return and get a **full refund** of taxes paid
- Law 144/2025 introduced a **15% flat tax** on overtime/shift premiums

## 🛂 Post-Study Work Permit

### Key Changes (Law 187/2024 – Cutro Decree) 🚀

| What Changed | Old System | New System (2025–2026) |
|-------------|------------|----------------------|
| **Work permit conversion** | Had to wait for annual "Decreto Flussi" quotas | **No quotas! Convert anytime** once you have a job offer |
| **Timing** | Could only convert after graduation | Can convert **at any time** before your permit expires |
| **Job search visa** | — | **12 months** after graduation (Permesso di Soggiorno per Ricerca Lavoro) |

### EU Blue Card (Revised 2025)
- For **highly qualified workers under 30**
- Minimum salary threshold: **€28,000 gross/year** (lowered from previous amount)
- Easier to switch employers
- Faster path to long-term residency

### Path Options After Graduation
1. **Job Search Visa** — 12 months to find a job
2. **Direct Work Permit** — If you find a job, convert immediately (no quota)
3. **EU Blue Card** — For highly qualified roles (€28k+ salary)
4. **Self-Employment** — Convert to Lavoro Autonomo (freelance/self-employment permit)

## 🏠 Permanent Residency (PR)

### Requirements
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Minimum residence** | 5 years of continuous, legal residence |
| **Study years count?** | ⚠️ Yes, but at **50% discount** (2 years study = 1 year toward PR) |
| **Income requirement** | Must earn at least **Assegno Sociale** (€7,101.12 for 2026) |
| **Language test** | **A2 Italian** — mandatory |
| **Can it be revoked?** | Yes, if you leave the EU for 12+ consecutive months |

### How Study Years Are Counted
- 2 years on a student permit = **1 year** toward PR
- You typically need to **switch to a work permit** to complete the 5-year requirement

### Benefits of PR
- Indefinite residency rights
- Increased mobility within the EU
- Can be lost if outside EU for 12+ months

- A registered contract is required for: **Permesso di Soggiorno** + **19% tax deduction on rent**
- **Deposit:** 2–3 months of rent (Caparra)
- Avoid scams: Never pay without a signed, registered contract
- Airbnb is accepted for **initial 30 days** only — a long-term lease is safer for visa

### DSU Housing
- DSU scholarship winners get either a **dorm bed** or a **cash equivalent** to rent privately
- Without DSU, finding affordable housing in Milan/Rome is very challenging
